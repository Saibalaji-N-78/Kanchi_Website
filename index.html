The City of Temples

Kanchipuram, also known as Kanchi, is one of the seven sacred cities of India and holds immense religious significance in Hinduism. This ancient city served as the capital of the Pallava dynasty and is renowned for its magnificent temples, each representing unique architectural styles and spiritual traditions.

The city is divided into two parts - Shiva Kanchi (Big Kanchi) and Vishnu Kanchi (Little Kanchi), representing the harmonious coexistence of different Hindu traditions. Beyond its spiritual significance, Kanchipuram is also famous for its exquisite silk sarees, traditional crafts, and warm hospitality.

  • 1000+ Temples: Ancient shrines dating back to the Pallava era
  • UNESCO Heritage: Architectural marvels of Dravidian style
  • Silk Capital: World-famous Kanchipuram silk sarees
  • Festivals: Year-round celebrations and spiritual events
  • History: Over 2000 years of continuous worship
  • Accessibility: Well-connected by road and rail
