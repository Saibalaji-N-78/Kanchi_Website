The Historical Significance of Kanchipuram

Kanchi has many historical monuments and temples considered to be the seat of education in ancient India. For this, it was also known as the ghatikasthanam, or "place of learning". The term "Ghatikasthanam" literally means "the place of learning" in Sanskrit, and Kanchipuram has proudly held this title for over two thousand years.

Between the first and fifth centuries, the city, also known as Ghatikasthanam (place of learning), was a religious center of advanced education for Jainism and Buddhism. The city as an educational hub is mentioned in various inscriptions like the Kadamba copper plate of the fifth century. And it was not just a centre for Vedic education but also an active Jain and Buddhist centre of advanced education from the 1st to the 5th centuries.

Ghatikasthanam is derived from the Sanskrit word "Ghatika" (meaning a period of time equivalent to 24 minutes) and "Sthanam" (meaning place). It refers to a place where systematic learning took place in structured time periods, making it one of the world's earliest organized educational institutions.

Early References

Kanchipuram finds mention in Patanjali's Mahabhashya, establishing its ancient heritage and scholarly importance.

1st - 5th Century CE

Multi-Religious Learning Hub

The city became a major center for advanced education in Buddhism, Jainism, and Vedic studies, attracting scholars from across Asia.

5th - 8th Century CE

Pallava Patronage

Under the Pallava dynasty, Kanchipuram reached its golden age as a center of learning, art, and architecture.

8th - 13th Century CE

Philosophical Renaissance

Great philosophers like Adi Sankara and Ramanuja studied and taught here, making it a center of Hindu philosophical thought.

SCSVMV University: Modern Continuation of Ancient Tradition

Sri Chandrasekharendra Saraswathi Viswa Mahavidyalaya

Established in 1993, SCSVMV University stands as a modern embodiment of Kanchipuram's ancient role as Ghatikasthanam. The university continues the city's millennium-old tradition of being a center of learning and knowledge dissemination.

The Continuing Legacy

Today, Kanchipuram continues its role as Ghatikasthanam through institutions like SCSVMV University. The Sri Chandrasekharendra Saraswathi International Library stands as a testament to this continuing tradition, serving as a modern repository of knowledge that bridges ancient wisdom with contemporary learning.

Just as ancient scholars traveled from distant lands to study in the original Ghatikasthanam, today's students and researchers come to SCSVMV University to access its extensive library resources and academic programs. The library's collection of over 200,000 books, along with digital resources and international journals, ensures that the tradition of learning continues to thrive in the 21st century.

The university offers 53 courses across 9 streams, maintaining the multi-disciplinary approach that made ancient Kanchipuram famous. From engineering and management to arts and sciences, the institution embodies the holistic educational philosophy that has made Kanchipuram synonymous with learning for over two millennia.
